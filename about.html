About the App

Addressing the Financial Crisis in Orphanages India has over 10,000 orphanages, many of which are facing closure due to severe financial shortages, leaving thousands of children at risk of losing essential care, education, and shelter. Our project provides a revolutionary solution by leveraging unused storage space on smartphones to generate sustainable funding. This model eliminates the need for direct monetary contributions, making it accessible to a broader audience while addressing the critical needs of orphanages. Leveraging Chia Blockchain for Eco-Friendly Fund Generation Our app utilizes Chia Blockchain's innovative Proof of Space and Time mechanism, an eco-friendly and energy-efficient alternative to traditional mining methods. Users simply allow the app to access 1% of their unused storage, which generates funds without consuming computational resources or affecting device performance. Transparency, Security, and Automation Through Blockchain Transparency and trust are central to our solution. Blockchain technology ensures all transactions are secure, immutable, and visible to users, fostering confidence in the system. A real-time dashboard allows contributors to monitor their impact, while smart contracts automate fund distribution directly to verified orphanages. These contracts allocate funds based on predefined criteria, such as the number of children and specific financial requirements, ensuring resources reach those who need them most. Engaging Users Through Rewards and Impactful Participation To encourage user participation, we introduce a reward system where contributors earn revert points for their contributions. These points can be redeemed for benefits such as prioritized ad placements or partner rewards, redefining the act of donation by creating tangible value for users. By combining eco-friendly blockchain technology, transparent fund allocation, and engaging user incentives, our project offers a scalable and sustainable solution to transform the funding landscape for orphanages in India.
